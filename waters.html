Chapter Three

The Waters

We run the lakes around Phillips, in Price County, one of the quieter corners of the Wisconsin Northwoods. Some are made for cruising and swimming, some are best left to the fishing. Here's what to know about each.

Not to scale. The actual chain is more generous on the south end and a bit more winding through the middle.

L1

Long Lake

419 acres and 54 feet at its deepest. Abundant walleye, with panfish and muskie fairly common. Two sandbars to pull up to, swim, and soak in the day.

Walleye · Muskie · Panfish 419 ac · Max 54'
L2

Duroy

350 acres and 18 feet deep, stumpy and weedy, best left to the anglers. Musky, walleye, northern pike, both bass, and panfish all hold here.

Musky · Walleye · Bass 350 ac · Max 18'
L3

Wilson

348 acres and 11 feet deep, reached from public landings. A good cruising lake with musky, walleye, northern pike, largemouth bass, and panfish.

Musky · Walleye · Bass 348 ac · Max 11'
L4

Elk

87 acres and 25 feet deep, with a public landing and beach. On the stumpy, weedy side and best for fishing — musky, smallmouth, walleye, northern pike, and panfish.

Musky · Smallmouth · Walleye 87 ac · Max 25'

Our home water

Solberg Lake

844 acres and 16 feet deep, the lake we call home, minutes from our place. Public landings, public beaches, and a county campground with two brand-new playgrounds. Water clarity runs low. Musky, walleye, largemouth bass, northern pike, and panfish.

Walleye · Musky · Bass 844 ac · Max 16' Our home lake

Above & Beside the Water

Six things worth doing
when the boat is on the trailer.

All within a short drive of Phillips. Ask us for recommendations when you pick up the boat.

Folk Art

Wisconsin Concrete Park

Fred Smith's 237-figure outdoor folk-art masterwork, right in Phillips. Bring an hour. Bring two.

Free · Open daily

High Ground

Timm's Hill

The highest natural point in Wisconsin — 1,951 ft. The view from the tower stretches all the way past the lakes you'll be on tomorrow.

35-min drive · Free

Trail

Smith Rapids Bridge

A covered bridge over the South Fork of the Flambeau, deep in the Chequamegon. Cold, fast water below it; pine canopy above.

45-min drive · Free

Park

Solberg Lake County Park

Sand beach, shaded grills, decent restrooms, a good fishing pier. A two-pontoon day usually ends here.

10-min drive · $5 day-use

Supper

Friday Fish Fry

The Sandtrap on Long Lake and Sunset Bay over on Solberg are our two picks. Both sit right on the water, so you can cruise over, tie up at the dock, and walk in for dinner.

Dockside · Fri only

Forest

Chequamegon-Nicolet

1.5 million acres of national forest start ten minutes north of town. Old-growth hemlock, blueberries in late July, no cell signal.

15-min drive · Free
