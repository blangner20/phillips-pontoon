Chapter Four

Rules of the Lake

The short version: be kind to the boat, be kinder to the lake, and read these once before you sign the rental agreement.

The full version lives in your confirmation email and rental agreement.

i.

Who can drive a boat

  • 01

    All renters must be 21 or older with a valid driver's license and a major credit card.

  • 02

    Wisconsin requires anyone born on or after January 1, 1989 to have a boater safety certificate to operate a motorized boat. Bring it or take it online at the DNR.

  • 03

    No one under 16 may operate the pontoon or fishing boat under any circumstance.

ii.

Safety on the water

  • 04

    A Coast Guard-approved life jacket for every person on board — non-negotiable. Children under 13 wear theirs whenever the boat is moving.

  • 05

    No alcohol while operating. We are happy to be the bad guy on this one. Wisconsin OUI laws apply on the water just like the road.

  • 06

    If the weather turns — lightning, sustained whitecaps, fog you can't see the next shoreline through — head in. We don't fine you for coming back early.

iii.

Fishing & licenses

  • 07

    Anyone 16 or older needs a Wisconsin fishing license. One-day, four-day, and annual licenses are available at dnr.wisconsin.gov.

  • 08

    Muskie season opens the first Saturday in May. Catch-and-release is encouraged on anything over 40". If you don't know what you're holding, take a photo and put it back.

  • 09

    Live bait must come from a Wisconsin-licensed dealer.

iv.

Money, fuel, and damage

  • 10

    Rentals are paid in full at booking. Cancel more than 14 days out for a 97% refund (less a $25 admin fee); inside 14 days you'll get a rental voucher; inside 72 hours, no refund or voucher.

  • 11

    Pontoon and fishing boat go out with a full tank. Bring them back full or we'll fill them at the dock at $5.49/gallon, plus a $10 refueling service fee.

  • 12

    A hold goes on your card at pickup for damage. We release it within 48 hours once everything comes back the way it left.

  • 13

    A rinse-down is included. A deep clean (food, bait, things that shouldn't be in a boat) is a $100 minimum.

  • 16

    Late returns get a 15-minute grace period, then $25 (16–30 min), $50 (31–60 min), or $100 plus the $30/hour extension rate past an hour.

  • 17

    Delivery and pickup to any public landing within 30 miles of Phillips (areas like Park Falls, Prentice, and Butternut) is a flat $80, on rentals of three days or more. Delivery runs any time Friday through Sunday, and after 4 PM Monday through Thursday.

v.

The lake itself

  • 14

    Slow-no-wake within 100 feet of any shoreline, dock, swimmer, or other boat. The loons will thank you. So will the loons' neighbors.

  • 15

    Drain everything before you cross to a different lake. Wisconsin invasive-species rules — Eurasian milfoil is real and it ruins things.

  • 18

    Pack out what you brought. If you're the kind of person who picks up someone else's stuff, too — there's an extra cookie at check-in.
